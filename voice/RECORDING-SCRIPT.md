# Voice recording script

Goal: capture 2 to 3 minutes of your natural voice so we can either (a) clone it (ElevenLabs
Instant Voice Clone or Higgsfield) for the video voiceover, or (b) use it as the reference while
you record the final voiceover yourself. Either way, these samples teach the model your cadence.

## How to record
- Quiet room, phone or laptop mic at arm's length, no music, no background noise.
- One block per take is fine. Speak the way you talk to a teammate, not like an announcer.
- Save as .m4a or .wav. Drop the files in `voice/samples/` (git-ignored) and tell me when they are in.
- Total target: 2 to 3 minutes of clean speech. More clean audio = a better clone.

## Block A - the video voiceover lines (English)
These are the exact lines the 60-second cut can use. Read them flat and confident, not hyped.

> "This is my agent fleet. Eight specialists, one orchestrator. The routing is tested in CI."

> "One task in. The orchestrator plans and fans out."

> "Four specialists, parallel. Each with its own scope and gates. I steered. I didn't type code."

> "It synthesizes, the gates pass, it opens the PR. Not a side experiment. It's how every feature ships."

## Block B - tone range (English)

> infrastructure, at an average cycle time under eighteen hours. The fleet writes the migration,
> the route, the component, and the decision record in parallel while I review and approve."

> "I automated my own job. Not as a layoff plan. As a career plan. The faster I make the version
